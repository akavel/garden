# Your value as an artist

([via u/buttpizz on r/printmaking](https://old.reddit.com/r/printmaking/comments/1u9ad62/-/osfe5md/))

> To add to this, your value as an artist is that you are taking your ideas and
> bringing them to life. That’s a blessing very few people have. You’re showing
> us, as your consumers, what you find to be compelling, interesting, or
> beautiful and then expressing that through your medium. It is so cool for us
> to witness that.
>
> When I would get frustrated with the quality of my work, it was because the
> image I had in my mind wasn’t accurately represented through my hands. Then I
> would push myself to be better, nothing would change, and then I would burn
> out. I think you can go beyond inadequacy and find an even deeper conflict.
>
> For me, I felt like people (friends, family) wouldn’t value my work if it
> wasn’t exceptionally crafted. But why would you want to convince loved ones
> that your mind is worth appreciating? When I joined art clubs and was exposed
> to people who were absolutely fascinated with my mind, I stopped caring
> completely about quality, and what unsupportive people thought, and started
> caring about the unique perspectives and stories that my mind could tell.
